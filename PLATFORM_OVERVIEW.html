Complete Journey — 10 Stages
1
STAGE 1 Register & Build Profile
Done
Signs up with name, email, occupation, education level, work experience years, German language level (A1–B2), Indian state & district, and passport availability. Profile completeness contributes directly to Readiness Score.
Registration form Occupation selection (20+ trades) State/district geo-picker Language self-assessment
2
STAGE 2 View AI Roadmap
AI FeatureDone
The AI Roadmap page generates a personalized, phased migration plan based on occupation, language level, and location. Divided into 3–5 phases with a realistic timeline, costs in INR and EUR, and specific next actions per phase.
Phase-wise breakdown Costs: INR + EUR Timeline: weeks per phase Government scheme links POST /api/ai/roadmap
3
STAGE 3 Check Readiness Score
Active
The Readiness Score page shows a weighted 8-factor eligibility score (0–100). Each factor has a weight and the candidate sees their gap areas. Score gates stage unlocking — below threshold, later stages stay locked.
Profile completeness 10% Identity documents 15% Passport validity 10% Language level 20% Qualification recognition 15% Work experience 10% Job match 5%
4
STAGE 4 Upload Documents to Vault
Active
Candidate uploads passport copy, educational certificates, work experience letters, and language certificates to the Document Vault. Each document goes through admin verification. Expiry dates are tracked. Unverified critical documents block visa stage.
Category-wise upload Verification status badge Expiry date alert Admin review workflow
5
STAGE 5 Enroll in Language Training
Pending
Browses the Training page to find A1–B2 German courses from accredited institutes (Goethe-Institut, Max Mueller Bhavan, etc.). Enrolls in a batch — online, offline, or hybrid. Attendance and assessment scores are tracked. Certificate issuance unlocks Stage 5.
Course marketplace Batch scheduling Attendance tracking Certificate issued flag
6
STAGE 6 Open Recognition Case
Pending
Creates a Qualification Recognition case on the Recognition page. Facilitator handles the Anerkennungsberatung (recognition advisory) and ZAB equivalency process. Candidate tracks decision status — full, partial, or not recognized. Full recognition is required for licensed professions (nursing, engineering).
Case creation Authority assignment Document checklist Decision: full/partial/not recognized Appeal management
7
STAGE 7 Browse Vacancies & Apply
Unlocks after Stage 5+6
Once language and recognition milestones are achieved, the Applications page activates. Candidate sees matched vacancies ranked by platform match score (occupation, language, experience). One-click apply creates an application record visible to the employer.
AI match score per vacancy Salary range visible Visa support indicator Application status tracking
8
STAGE 8 Interview & Receive Offer
Pending
Employer schedules an interview (video or in-person). Candidate attends and receives outcome on the Applications page. On success, employer issues a formal job offer — salary, start date, visa support confirmation. Candidate accepts or declines digitally on-platform.
Interview date & mode Outcome recording Offer letter details Digital acceptance
9
STAGE 9 Visa Application
Locked — needs offer
After an accepted job offer, the Visa page activates. Candidate opens a National Visa (Type D) or Chancenkarte case. A 10-item checklist tracks: passport validity, signed offer letter, recognition result, language certificate, health insurance, embassy appointment, etc. Facilitator assists throughout.
10-item visa checklist Embassy appointment tracking Document submission status Visa granted / rejected outcome
10
STAGE 10 Post-Arrival Welfare
Goal
After arrival in Germany, the Welfare page lets candidates raise support tickets for integration issues: housing, healthcare, legal advice, cultural adjustment, employer disputes, or financial assistance. Facilitators are assigned tickets and resolve them with documented notes. Priority levels control response time.
Issue categories: housing/health/legal/financial Priority: urgent / normal / low Facilitator assignment Resolution + closure notes

Employer Hiring Flow — 6 Steps
1
STEP 1Set Up Company Profile
One-time
HR admin fills in company details: name, industry/sector, city, state (Germany), company size, visa sponsorship capability, accommodation support availability, and contact details. Profile is verified by Platform Admin before going live.
Sector + cityVisa sponsorship flagAccommodation support flagAdmin verification
2
STEP 2Post Vacancies
Creates job postings on the Vacancies page. Each vacancy specifies: occupation category, required language level (A1–B2), minimum work experience years, salary range (EUR), joining date, recognition required flag, visa support, and accommodation support. Posted vacancies immediately enter the matching algorithm.
Occupation + language requirementSalary min/maxJoining dateRecognition required flag
3
STEP 3Review Matched Candidates
AI Matching
The Matches page shows candidates ranked by platform match score for each vacancy. Employer reviews candidate profiles via the global slide-out profile card — seeing occupation, language level, readiness score, journey stage, documents, and experience. Can shortlist or pass.
AI match score per candidateSlide-out profile cardReadiness score visibleJourney stage visible
4
STEP 4Process Applications
Applications page shows all inbound applications for the company's vacancies. HR can accept or reject applications with notes. Accepted applications move to interview scheduling. Candidates see application status updates on their side in real-time.
Status: pending → accepted / rejectedNotes per decisionCandidate notified automatically
5
STEP 5Schedule Interviews
Interviews page allows scheduling with date/time, mode (video call or in-person), and notes. After the interview, HR records the outcome (pass/fail/hold). Passed interviews trigger offer creation flow.
Date, time, modeInterviewer notesOutcome: pass / fail / hold
6
STEP 6Issue Job Offer
Hire Complete
Creates a formal job offer on the Offers page: salary (EUR/year), start date, visa support confirmation, and accommodation support. Candidate receives offer and accepts or declines on their platform. Accepted offer triggers Visa stage unlock on the candidate's side.
Salary + start dateVisa sponsorship confirmationCandidate acceptance trackingTriggers visa stage

Facilitator Case Management Flow
1
TASK 1Monitor All Candidates
The Candidates page shows every registered candidate across the platform with name, occupation, current journey stage, readiness score, and any flags. Facilitator can click any candidate to see full profile via the universal profile slide-out card. Can filter by stage, state, occupation, or score range.
Full candidate rosterFilter by stage/score/stateSlide-out profile viewStage flags visible
2
TASK 2Handle Recognition Cases
Critical Path
Facilitator manages all open recognition cases on the Recognition Cases page. For each case: assigns the competent recognition authority (ZAB, relevant state authority, or professional chamber), tracks document submission, monitors decision progress (submitted → under review → decision). Decision types: full equivalency, partial, adaptation measure required, or not recognized.
Authority assignmentDocument checklist trackingStatus: submitted → review → decisionDecision type recordingAppeal option
3
TASK 3Manage Visa Cases
Visa Cases page shows all open National Visa and Chancenkarte applications. Facilitator tracks consulate appointment dates, ensures all 10 checklist items are completed, uploads missing documents, adds embassy correspondence notes, and records the final visa decision. Critical blocker resolution is the facilitator's primary job here.
Visa type: National D / ChancenkarteAppointment date tracking10-item checklist statusEmbassy notes logDecision: granted / rejected
4
TASK 4Resolve Welfare Tickets
Post-Arrival
Welfare Tickets page aggregates all open support tickets from candidates who have arrived in Germany. Facilitator assigns priority (urgent/normal/low), takes ownership, communicates resolution steps, and closes tickets with documented notes. Ticket categories: housing, healthcare, legal, cultural, employer dispute, financial.
Priority triageFacilitator assignmentCategory filteringResolution notesClosure tracking

AI Engine

4 AI Endpoints — Zero API Keys Required

All AI runs on rule-based logic in the Express backend. Smart enough to be useful, lightweight enough to deploy anywhere without external costs.

🤖

AI Chat Assistant

Floating assistant on every page. Responds to natural language queries with pre-scripted contextual answers matched by keyword trees. Returns suggestions and deep-links.

POST/api/ai/chat
Input: { message, context }
Output: { response, suggestions[], links[] }
🗺️

Personalized Roadmap

Generates occupation-specific, phased migration plans with realistic cost breakdowns in INR and EUR based on Indian city, occupation, and current language level.

POST/api/ai/roadmap
Input: { occupation, languageLevel, state }
Output: { phases[], totalMonths, costINR, costEUR }
📄

Resume Advisor

Occupation-specific CV improvement system. Matches resume sections against German standards and ATS requirements. Returns a numeric ATS compatibility score and prioritized improvements.

POST/api/ai/resume-suggest
Input: { occupation, resumeText }
Output: { atsScore, improvements[], tips[] }
🗺️

Geographic Reference

Returns the complete list of 20 Indian states each with their districts for candidate registration forms, filtering, and regional analytics.

GET/api/geo/states
Output: { states: [{ name, districts[] }] }
